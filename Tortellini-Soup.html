Tortellini Soup

If you're looking for a decently quick and effortless meal after a rough day learning some new code well look no further than my recipe for Tortellini soup. Something you can just do very little prep. Toss a bunch of ingredients into a pot, get it boiling and then simmer until you're ready to eat. A real meal for someone who wants to shut off their brain for a minute and relax while letting the stove top do all the work.

Equipment needed

  • Cutting Board
  • Big pot
  • knife

Ingredients

  • 1 Tablespoon Olive Oil
  • 1 Tablespoon Butter
  • 1 onion (diced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • Red pepper flakes to taste
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 14 ounce can diced tomatoes
  • 8 ounce tomato sauce
  • 1 Teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon Italian Seasoning
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 ounces cheese torellini (refidgerated kind)
  • 2 cups of spinach leaves
  • 1 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • half cup basil leaves (chopped)

Instructions

  1. Add oil and butter to a large saucepan over medium heat. Once melted, add the onion and cook, stirring occasionally, 5 minutes.
  2. Add the garlic. Stir in the flour and cook for another minute.
  3. Add the vegetable broth, diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, Italian seasoning, and dried basil. Season with salt and pepper. Taste broth and adjust seasonings if needed.
  4. Bring mixture to a gentle boil and then add the tortellini. Cook for a few minutes, until tortellini are tender.
  5. Remove from heat and stir in spinach, parmesan cheese, and cream.
  6. Add chopped basil. Serve warm.
